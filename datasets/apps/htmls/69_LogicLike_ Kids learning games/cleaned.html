Cookies

Cookies are small pieces of data that are stored on your computer or mobile device. They contain information about your browsing activity. Whenever you visit the Logiclike website, it sends a cookie to the device you are using to access the website. Your device may automatically store the cookie in a file that is located within your web browser. When you revisit the Logiclike site, it will respond in a more personalized way, remembering your preferences. The latter is possible due to the corresponding cookies.

Logiclike website uses cookies to improve the performance of the site and the user experience it delivers. We use necessary and third-party cookies. Necessary cookies are designed by Logiclike, and third-party cookies - by the companies whose services we use in the Logiclike website and app. We will expand on each category of cookies below.

How does Logiclike use cookies?

Logiclike employs cookies of two types: session and persistent. Session cookies are temporary; they disappear when you leave the site or exit your browser. Persistent cookies remain in a cookie file on your device over the given time. Both types of cookies are used to enable the smooth and failure-free performance of Logiclike website and app. Cookies speed up page loads, enable requests for service, help automatically recognize you, enable safe connection, and many more. Session cookies help us understand your preferences when interacting with the site. Persistent cookies work for statistics and analytics, help us see a bigger picture of what is important to you on the web.

Necessary Cookies

Logiclike designs necessary cookies by themselves. These cookies are used by the Logiclike website. They help the site identify you as a user, authorize your logging into the site, block suspicious attempts to reach users' personal data, and bar using them for malicious purposes. Some necessary cookies track your browsing history and the use of apps. With due analyzes of these data, we are able to identify the shortcomings of Logiclike, and elaborate ways for improvement. This is, for example, how we understand the performance of the Logiclike app and website in various parts of the world, or how we diagnose bugs in the services we offer.

Third-Party Cookies

Logiclike website supports third-party cookies. These cookies further help us understand how you interact with our services - all for subsequent improvements thereof. Furthermore, for selected analyzes, we may also process other data that are collected by the third-party cookies. Some of the third-party cookies are marketing cookies. These help us show you relevant and timely announcements and notifications. We use marketing cookies by Google Analytics, Yandex Metrica, Facebook and Twitter. Statistical third-party cookies are used for analytical purposes: for example, performance analysis and business research. We use statistical cookies by Google Analytics, Segment and Branch.

How to manage and decline cookies

Most browsers will ask what you want to do with the cookies they use. We have collected a list of manuals (see below) on how to manage cookies in some most popular browsers.

• Google Chrome

• Internet Explorer

• Firefox
• Safari

• Opera

As a rule, the browser will display three alternatives whenever you land on the page: accept, decline or disable cookies. This may expand on all the cookies, third-party cookies or cookies from selected websites. Logiclike cookies, when declined, may limit the functionality of the Logiclike website and app, which will result in some services and features getting unavailable.

Do-Not-Track Signals

The Logiclike website and app may not interpret the Do-Not-Track (DNT) signals that your browser sends them. There is no consensus yet on how the companies one encounters while browsing should interpret DNT. Thereby we have refrained from the use of DNT considering the potential conflicts that may happen with some of the Logiclike features otherwise. Once there appears a universally-signed standard for what we should do when detecting a DNT signal, we will respond to the change appropriately.
